About Nowruz

Nowruz, meaning "New Day" in Persian, is the Iranian New Year celebrated on the spring equinox. This ancient festival marks the beginning of spring and symbolizes renewal, rebirth, and new beginnings. For over 3,000 years, millions of people across the world have celebrated this beautiful tradition.

The Iranian Student Association (ISA) at Georgia Tech is proud to bring this celebration to our campus community. Our annual Nowruz event features traditional Persian music, dance performances, authentic cuisine, and the iconic Haft-Sin table display. Join us as we celebrate the arrival of spring and share our rich cultural heritage with the Georgia Tech family.

Whether you're Persian or simply curious about Iranian culture, everyone is welcome to experience the joy and warmth of Nowruz with us!

The Festival of Fire

On the eve of the last Wednesday before Nowruz, Iranians celebrate Chaharshanbe Soori, an ancient fire festival with roots stretching back over 4,000 years to Zoroastrian traditions. The name means "Scarlet Wednesday," reflecting the brilliant red glow of bonfires that illuminate the night sky.

This mystical celebration marks the triumph of light over darkness, good over evil, and serves as a spiritual cleansing before the New Year begins.

The Longest Night of the Year

Yalda Night, celebrated on the winter solstice (December 21st), is one of the most ancient and cherished Persian traditions. The name "Yalda" comes from the Syriac word meaning "birth," celebrating the rebirth of the sun as days begin to grow longer after the longest night of the year.

In 2022, UNESCO recognized Yalda Night on its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, acknowledging its profound significance in Persian culture.
